A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

Missile man of india.

He had born on 15 October 1931.

Kalam had sold newspapers to supplement his family’s income.

He accomplished Aeronautical Engineering from Madras Institute of Technology in 1960.

His childhood dream was of becoming a fighter pilot.

He joined the Defence Research and Development Organization (DRDO) as a scientist after graduation.

He joined the Indian Space research Organization (ISRO) in 1969.

Kalam Sir served as the Project Director of Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle III (PSLV III).

His dexterity in space science made him popular as 'Missile Man of India'.

He was the 11th President of the Republic of India.

He took his last breath on 27th July 2015.

He penned down his autobiography named ‘Wings of Fire’ in the year 1999.

He became the President of India by the unanimous support of ruling as well as of opposition party.

He was the prestigious Bharat Ratna awardee in the year 1997.

A.P.J Abdul Kalam was famous as the People’s President.

His birthday on 15th October is Youth Renaissance Day in Tamilnadu.

Several educational institutions and awards have used the name Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam to pay him the respect.

In his lifetime Kalam had received seven honorary doctorates from forty universities.

n 2002, Kalam was 2nd in Outlook India’s poll on the greatest Indian after B.R. Ambedkar.

He got Bharat Ratna for his contribution to the Indian defense technology.

He served as the Scientific Adviser to the Government of India.

Despite being a non-Hindu, he had memorized Bhagwat Gita by heart.

As a child, Mr. Kalam used to study till late night in kerosene light to make his dreams come true.

The thirst for knowledge was the driving force behind the success of Kalam.

Uttar Pradesh Government has renamed the technical university as APJ Abdul Kalam Technical University.

Mr. Kalam had spent nearly four decades of his life as a scientist and science administrator.

Dr. Kalam also worked with renowned space scientist Vikram Sarabhai

Kalam developed a coronary stent with cardiologist Soma Raju, which was named “Kalam Raju stent”.

He won the 2002 Presidential elections with an electoral vote of 9, 22,884.

APJ Abdul Kalam swore as the 11th President of the Republic of India on 25th July 2002.

Mr. Kalam wrote many popular books based on the science and power of youth.

He played a pivotal role in Pokhran II, India’s nuclear test at Pokhran in 1998.

He also played a key role in the development of the Agni missile system for Indian Defense.
